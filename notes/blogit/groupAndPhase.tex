Definition: compact support

A signal that is time limited.  Outside of some range \( [a,b] \), the function is zero.

In order for a signal to be causal, there much be a range, outside of which, it is zero.  This can mean that there is a discontinity, or singularity, of the function or its derivatives at the boundaries.

The velocity of such singularites is exactly what we associate with the transmission of information.  We generally detect the group velocity by looking at the peak or envelope of the pulse.  If we were able to make the best possible detector, we would be looking at the propagation speed of the singularity(ies).
